# Load Management And Tendons

## Sources
- Breda SJ et al. Effectiveness of progressive tendon-loading exercise therapy in patellar tendinopathy: randomized clinical trial. British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2021. PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33219115/
- Exercise therapy for tendinopathy: evidence synthesis. NCBI Bookshelf: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK597137/
- Load progression criteria in exercise programmes in lower limb tendinopathy: systematic review. PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7678382/

## Practical Principles
- Tendons and irritated joints usually respond better to managed progressive loading than sudden load spikes.
- Pain signals should change exercise selection, range, tempo, volume, and progression speed.
- Load tolerance is built over weeks, not by forcing a single hard session.
- Heavy slow loading and progressive exposure are useful concepts, but the exact exercise depends on the tissue and athlete.

## Programming Rules
- If pain is present, do not increase both intensity and volume in the same week.
- Keep pain-sensitive movements submaximal and technically strict.
- Use tempo, range limits, or regressions to keep symptoms manageable while preserving training effect.
- Add explicit monitoring: pain during, pain after 24 hours, and performance trend.
- Avoid testing maximal holds or high-impact ballistic work when feedback suggests tendon flare or poor recovery.

## Use In SessionAI
- Generated programs must include conservative substitutions when pain is reported.
- If `pain` is `managed` or `present`, the rationale must describe how load was controlled.
