# Calisthenics Goal Rules

## Evidence Base
This file translates the broader strength, skill, isometric, and load-management evidence into practical calisthenics defaults. Use it with the primary science files, not as a standalone citation source.

## Front Lever
- Priority qualities: straight-arm pulling strength, scapular depression/retraction control, posterior chain body-line tension, trunk anti-extension.
- Typical frequency: 2-3 exposures per week.
- Main work: clean holds, raises, negatives, band-assisted holds, or regression holds.
- Hold target: mostly short clean exposures, often 6-12 seconds, avoiding form collapse.
- Rest: 120-240 seconds on key holds.
- Progression: reduce assistance, lengthen lever, add seconds, or add sets only when shape is consistent.

## Planche
- Priority qualities: straight-arm pushing, scapular protraction, wrist/shoulder tolerance, lean control.
- Progress slowly when wrist/shoulder feedback is negative.
- Put planche skill early and keep accessory pushing from destroying joint tolerance.

## Handstand Push-Up
- Priority qualities: vertical pressing strength, overhead mobility, handstand line, eccentric control.
- Separate balance/line practice from hard pressing when the athlete is inconsistent.
- Use video for line and depth on key sets.

## Muscle-Up
- Priority qualities: high pull strength, transition timing, dip support, false grip or bar path depending on variation.
- Avoid too many failed attempts; use transition drills and explosive pulls with quality.

## General Calisthenics Rule
- The main skill must appear before accessories.
- Accessories should support the bottleneck, not just add fatigue.
- The coach-facing rationale must state the selected bottleneck.
